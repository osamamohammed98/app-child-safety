Child Safety Standards – بالحلال

Effective Date: March 2026

بالحلال is a dating and communication platform strictly intended for adults aged 18 years and older. The application is developed and published by Bl7alal.com and complies with all applicable child safety and protection regulations.

1. Zero Tolerance for Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

بالحلال has a strict zero-tolerance policy against any form of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE). Any content, communication, behavior, or account involving minors in a sexual context is permanently banned.

We strictly prohibit the creation, upload, sharing, solicitation, or distribution of Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). Any suspected CSAM will be immediately removed and reported to relevant national and international authorities as required by law.

2. Adults Only (18+) Policy

Our platform is strictly limited to individuals who are 18 years of age or older. Users must confirm their age during registration.

  • Accounts suspected of belonging to minors are permanently removed.
  • We reserve the right to request additional verification where necessary.
  • Any attempt to misrepresent age will result in permanent account termination.

3. In‑App Reporting Mechanism

Users can report profiles, messages, images, or other content directly within the app using the built-in reporting feature.

  • Report options are available on user profiles.
  • Reports are available within chat conversations.
  • All reports are reviewed by our moderation team.

4. Moderation and Enforcement

We actively monitor user reports and enforce our community standards. When a report involving child safety concerns is received:

  • Content is reviewed immediately.
  • Accounts may be suspended during investigation.
  • Confirmed violations result in permanent bans.
  • Serious cases are reported to appropriate authorities.

5. Cooperation with Authorities

بالحلال complies with all applicable child protection laws and mandatory reporting obligations. We cooperate fully with law enforcement and child protection agencies when legally required.
